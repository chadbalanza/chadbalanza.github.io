Design Challenge 1

Which two websites are you most inspired by?

YourKarma (portable WiFi) - www.yourkarma.com

HUGE (digital agency) - www.hugeinc.com

How do they exhibit effective use of space, size or balance?

YourKarma utilizes a full width background video to tell the story of their product. Paired with a brief headline/subhead and a transparent nav bar, users are immediately engaged through the use of focus.

HUGE plays with negative space and unique imagery compositions on their homepage. They have condensed the nav to a menu icon and left the rest of the page empty to drive focus to the large image and project detail, center-justified.

What makes them similar and different?

Big image/small copy is a common theme. Both sites successfully grab the attention of users through effective use of space and scale. However, they drive engagement a bit differently. YourKarma relies on the video loop in the background to stimulate users' attention, while HUGE forces users to scroll the page. The aesthetic approach is very similar though - clean, minimal, and vibrant.

Thumbnail Sketches

The following sketches are for a portfolio site.

  • Homepage v1

    • The idea for this version is to lead in with bold headlines-an emphasis on typography vs imagery. Use of negative space and balance will provide clear focal points. Project grid below hero banner serves as a preview into portfolio, and as a page divider between content sections.

  • Homepage v2

    • Large full width image for big impact in version 2. Use of scale will provide focus on copy, and a transparent navigation bar keeps page clear of visual distractions. Imagery bleeds to edge of browser window.

  • About v1

    • Continuation of bold typography theme. Leads into 2-column section: copy on left, image grid on right. There is a balance of copy content to images, which helps create a nice flow through the page.

  • About v2

    • Minimalist approach in this version. Lead in with large hero image and short headline, followed by small image and copy. Everything center justified, to mimic homepage structure.

  • Portfolio Page v1

    • Very similar page layout (as "About v1" above). Project title up top, content below. Images of project will be main focal points throughout page.

  • Portfolio Page v2

    • Heirarchy is key in this version. Eye starts at top hero image/headline, follows the page down to body copy, and finally through a grid layout of images.
